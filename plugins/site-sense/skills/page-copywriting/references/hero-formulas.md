# Hero formulas

A good hero answers: what is it, who is it for, what changes, and what to do next.

## Clear formula

```text
[Product/category] for [audience] who need [outcome] without [friction].
```

Use when category clarity matters.

## Outcome formula

```text
Get from [before] to [after] with [mechanism/product].
```

Use when transformation is more important than category.

## Problem-first formula

```text
[Problem] is costing [audience] [cost]. [Product] helps fix it by [mechanism].
```

Use for pain-aware users.

## Proof-led formula

```text
[Proof/result] for [similar audience] — built into [product/service].
```

Use when skepticism is high.

## Alternative formula

```text
A better way to [job] than [alternative]: [main difference].
```

Use in crowded markets.

## Local/service formula

```text
[Service] for [audience/location] with [specific process/proof] and [clear next step].
```

## Hero components

- Eyebrow: optional category/context.
- H1: main claim.
- Subheading: explanation and mechanism.
- Primary CTA: high-intent action.
- Secondary CTA: lower-friction action.
- Proof strip: logos, number, quote, credential, or process.

## Anti-patterns

- «Инновационные решения для вашего бизнеса».
- «Выведите бизнес на новый уровень».
- H1 that could fit any competitor.
- CTA that says only «Подробнее».
- Claim with no audience and no result.
