# Segment card

Use one card per meaningful audience segment. Do not create segments by demographics alone; segment by situation, job, constraints, buying role, and trigger event.

```markdown
## Segment: [name]

### Situation

- What is happening when they start looking?
- What changed recently?
- What pressure are they under?

### Job to be done

When [situation], they want to [make progress], so they can [outcome].

### Current alternatives

- Direct competitors:
- Indirect alternatives:
- Manual workaround:
- Doing nothing:

### Buying context

- Buyer:
- User:
- Influencers:
- Approval blockers:
- Budget owner:
- Timeline:

### Decision criteria

| Criterion | Why it matters | How to prove it |
|---|---|---|

### Objections

| Objection | Root cause | Needed answer |
|---|---|---|

### Messages that work

- Main promise:
- Mechanism:
- Proof:
- CTA:

### Messages to avoid

- [Phrase] — why it will not work.
```

## Segment priority score

Rate 1–5:

- urgency;
- willingness to pay;
- access/reach;
- product fit;
- proof available;
- strategic value.

Prioritize segments with high urgency, high product fit, and strong proof.
