# Proof inventory

Separate claims from proof. A claim without proof is either a hypothesis, a weak promise, or a future research task.

## Proof types

| Proof type | Examples | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Direct result | revenue, conversion, speed, cost, error reduction | Strong |
| Customer evidence | testimonials, case studies, logos, quotes | Strong |
| Product evidence | screenshots, demos, before/after, workflow | Medium-strong |
| Process evidence | methodology, standards, QA, onboarding | Medium |
| Authority evidence | certifications, awards, expert credentials | Medium |
| Risk reducer | guarantees, trial, SLA, support, security notes | Medium |
| Unsupported assertion | «лучший», «надёжный», «качественный» | Weak |

## Claim/proof table

```markdown
| Claim | Audience segment | Proof available | Proof strength 1-5 | Where to use | Risk note |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---|

```

## Rules

- Put proof near the claim, not only at the bottom of the page.
- Do not amplify a claim beyond the evidence.
- If proof is missing, write a placeholder: `[нужен кейс / цифра / пример]`.
- For regulated or high-risk topics, avoid absolute promises unless verified.
- Prefer concrete examples over generic trust badges.

## Common proof gaps

- Claim says «быстрее», but no baseline.
- Claim says «экономит», but no calculation.
- Claim says «под ключ», but no scope.
- Claim says «экспертно», but no cases or credentials.
- Claim says «без риска», but no guarantee, SLA, or process.
