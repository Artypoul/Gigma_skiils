# Buyer journey map

Map content by what the audience is trying to decide, not by what the company wants to publish.

| Stage | User question | Content types | CTA | Common mistake |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | What is the problem and why does it matter? | guides, checklists, explainers, problem pages | read related guide, download template | too generic, no product connection |
| Consideration | What approaches or tools can solve it? | solution pages, comparisons, frameworks, webinars | view solution, compare options | pretending alternatives do not exist |
| Decision | Why choose this vendor/product? | pricing, case studies, demos, security, implementation | book demo, start trial, request quote | weak proof and vague CTA |
| Implementation | How do we make it work? | docs, onboarding, tutorials, migration guides | setup, contact support, invite team | no handoff from sale to adoption |
| Expansion/retention | How do we get more value? | advanced guides, templates, release notes, best practices | upgrade, expand use case | only acquisition content |

## Content balance rule

A healthy strategy usually needs:

- acquisition content for demand;
- decision content for conversion;
- support content for adoption;
- proof content for trust;
- opinion/research content for differentiation.

## Questions to generate topics

### Awareness

- What symptoms does the audience notice first?
- What mistakes do they make before seeking help?
- What vocabulary do they use before knowing the category?

### Consideration

- What alternatives do they compare?
- What criteria matter most?
- What trade-offs are confusing?

### Decision

- What proof reduces risk?
- What implementation doubts block buying?
- What objections show up in sales calls?

### Implementation

- What does the user need after conversion?
- What setup steps create friction?
- What templates or examples make success easier?
