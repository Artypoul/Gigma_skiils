# Editorial roadmap

Use this to prioritize content by business relevance, effort, confidence, and dependency order.

## Prioritization score

Rate each 1–5.

| Factor | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Business impact | How close topic is to revenue, retention, or strategic trust |
| Audience urgency | How painful/timely the topic is |
| Search opportunity | Evidence of demand or internal site search |
| Product fit | Natural connection to what we sell |
| Proof availability | Cases, examples, expertise, data available |
| Effort | Lower score if hard; invert effort when calculating |
| Confidence | How sure we are about audience/intent |

Simple formula:

```text
Priority = impact + urgency + search + product_fit + proof + confidence - effort
```

## Roadmap template

```markdown
| Week | Asset | Type | Cluster | Owner | Source material | CTA | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Title] | Hub / post / case / page | [Cluster] | [Owner] | [Inputs] | [CTA] | Planned |
```

## 4-week starter roadmap

### Week 1

- one high-intent product/service page;
- one proof asset or case study;
- one internal linking update.

### Week 2

- one hub page or guide;
- two supporting posts or templates;
- one conversion FAQ update.

### Week 3

- one comparison or alternatives page;
- one implementation guide;
- update older content with links.

### Week 4

- measure early results;
- refresh titles/descriptions;
- expand best-performing cluster;
- collect missing proof.

## Governance

- Every content item must have a target audience, intent, CTA, and owner.
- Assign a refresh date for evergreen pages.
- Record assumptions and source material.
- Do not publish orphan content.
