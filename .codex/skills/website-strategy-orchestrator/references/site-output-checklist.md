# Site output checklist

Use this before calling the website package ready for design, development, or publication.

## Strategy

- [ ] Product category is clear.
- [ ] Primary audience is not described as «все».
- [ ] JTBD or desired progress is specific.
- [ ] Main promise is understandable in one screen.
- [ ] Differentiation names an alternative and a reason to switch.
- [ ] Strong claims have proof or are marked as hypotheses.

## Information architecture

- [ ] Sitemap has homepage, core conversion pages, trust pages, resource/support pages, and legal pages where needed.
- [ ] Navigation follows user tasks, not internal departments.
- [ ] Important pages are reachable within 1–3 clicks.
- [ ] URL patterns are stable, readable, lowercase, and hyphenated.
- [ ] Breadcrumbs, footer, and contextual links are specified.
- [ ] Redesign projects include redirect map and pages that must keep URLs.

## SEO and content

- [ ] Each SEO page has one primary intent.
- [ ] Title, description, H1, H2/H3, FAQ, schema candidates, and internal links are specified.
- [ ] Content clusters connect hub pages and supporting pages.
- [ ] Product tie-in is natural, not forced.
- [ ] Research gaps are listed separately from assumptions.

## Copy

- [ ] Hero says what it is, for whom, and what changes.
- [ ] Benefits are outcomes, not feature names.
- [ ] Objections are answered before the final CTA.
- [ ] CTA tells what happens after the click.
- [ ] Generic claims are removed or supported by proof.
- [ ] Russian copy is clean, direct, and not AI-generic.
